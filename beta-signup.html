Become a Pioneer

I'm building MindTheClub — a messenger where your messages travel directly between devices, never through a server. No accounts, no phone numbers, no cloud storage. Just a direct, encrypted connection between you and the person you're talking to.

I built every part of this app myself, from the peer-to-peer protocol to the group messaging system. Now I need real people, on real devices, in real network conditions, to help me find what I can't find alone.

What you're joining

MindTheClub is live on Google Play as an Open Testing release. This means you can install and use it right now — but you're joining as a tester, not a customer. There is a limit for Open Testing to 1,000 testers, so every spot matters.

What to expect

This is a beta. The app works — messaging, calls, file sharing, group chats — but you will encounter rough edges. A message might take longer to deliver when a device wakes from deep sleep. A connection might need a second attempt on a restrictive network. The UI might not feel polished in every corner. That's exactly why I need you.

What I need from you

1
Use it with someone The app is peer-to-peer — you need at least one other person to test with. Add a friend, a partner, a family member. The more contacts you add, the more valuable your testing becomes.
2
Test what matters Send messages when the other person's phone is locked. Try group chats with 3 or more people. Make a video call on Wi-Fi, then on mobile data. Send a large file. Use it the way you'd use any messenger — and notice when something doesn't feel right.
3
Tell me what breaks If something goes wrong, please let me know using the Contact Form. The more detail you include — what you were doing, what you expected, what happened instead — the faster I can fix it.
